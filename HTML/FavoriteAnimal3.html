Bio

The Snow Leopard live in the Mountains of Central Asia usually found at an altitude between 9,800-17,000 feet. Snow Leopards are Mammals and are Carnivores. They can grow up to be 4-5 feet long and weight 60-120 lbs.



Diet

The diet of the Snow Leopard consists mostly of Bharal and Alpine Ibex but they sometimes go for small rodents like a Marmot and hare or some birds. They are also know to hunt for livestocks in farms if the opportunity comes there way.



Endangered Status

From 1972 the Snow Leopard has been on the Endangered Species List do to climate change, hunters hunting them for the fur, and habitat lost do to farmers expanding their land. But as of December 1, 2017 they are no longer endangered. But even though they are not on the list of Endangered Species there poplation numbers are still low and will take time to recover.



Interesting Facts

The fur of the Snow Leopard is a natural insulator of their heat. In addition to that they have big feet that acts as snow shoes.
